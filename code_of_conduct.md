# Code of conduct

Contributions to this project keep their writing and history plain and
attributable. The rules below cover source, comments, documentation, commit
messages, and release notes.

## No em dashes

Do not use the em dash character (U+2014) anywhere: not in prose, not in code
comments, not in commit messages or changelog rows. Use a comma, a colon, a
period, or a plain hyphen with surrounding spaces instead.

## Minimal formatting

Keep formatting minimal. Write short, direct sentences. Reserve markup for
structure (headings, lists, tables, inline code) and avoid decorative
emphasis, emojis, and editorializing. Comment only where the intent is
non-obvious.

## No AI attribution

Do not attribute work to AI tools. Commit messages and release notes carry no
"Co-Authored-By" trailer for any machine or assistant, no "Generated with"
line, and no similar notice. The author of a change is the human who signs the
commit.
